ABOUT SIGN LANGUAGE

No, sign languages are unique and different in each country. Even within countries, regional sign languagevariations exist. Some countries have very well documented dictionaries and lexicons, which enable them to establish a national standard.
Sign languages, similar to spoken languages, often have a variety of dialects in different regions of the country. While many common words are shared throughout the country, some will be regional-specific. As sign language becomes standardized and in use throughout Pakistan, these variations tend to diminish. We have attempted to document the most commonly used sign for each word in this dictionary, in consultation with leading Deaf elders and educators nationwide.
The Ethnologue website (www.ethnologue.com) states that there are 7,105 known spoken languages and 138 documented sign languages.
Many of the words in Pakistan Sign Language utilize the two-handed, or manual, alphabet. The one-handed alphabet employed by American Sign Language has become the international standard and is recognizable nation-wide. Urdu finger spelling is not in common use.

ABOUT PSL RESOURCES

PSL Resources is a collection of:

  • The award-winning PSL Dictionary containing 7,000+ words.
  • 250 learning tutorials designed for Deaf education.
  • 45+ fun and engaging PSL stories - including the famous "Quaid se Baatein" – all culturally appropriate and with accompanying English & Urdu voice and subtitles.
  • 150 classroom tutorials for educators that demonstrate best practices and techniques.
  • The IQRA Literacy program – an innovative software that teaches reading and writing skills for deaf children.

All content is in line with Pakistan’s national curriculum, and has both English and Urdu voice over and subtitling.

A PSL Learning Unit (PLU) is a low cost, high impact solution to make PSL Resources easily available and accessible in classrooms as well as informal settings nationwide to capacitate teachers, students, and families.

Hardware: A Raspberry Pi minicomputer device with SD card and USB memory stick loaded with content is attached directly to a 22 inch LED monitor display. A keyboard and mouse are attached for utilization and navigation.

All the above listed PSL resources are loaded on the PLU allowing for offline access, and the internet is not required for usage.

Navigation of the PSL Learning Unit is designed to be as intuitive as possible. No prior computer knowledge is necessary to use.

The Units are highly portable. They can be used in classrooms with up to 30 students

Hundreds of PSL Learning Units have been distributed in schools, clubs, libraries, universities and institutes nationwide that work with the Deaf community.
